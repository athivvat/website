Moravec's paradox describes the counterintuitive phenomenon where tasks that are easy for humans, like walking or recognizing a face, are surprisingly difficult for machines to perform, while tasks that are intellectually challenging for humans, like complex mathematical calculations or playing chess, are relatively simple for computers.
